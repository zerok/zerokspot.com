During my recent trip to [Vancouver](https://www.reddit.com/r/vancouver/s/MLpW8agw5O) I noticed something that I've come to really love in [Portland, Oregon](https://www.reddit.com/r/Portland/s/0rcCrzNwAF): It is pretty much expected of you that you greet your bus driver when entering and thank them when leaving.

It's such a small gesture but it changes the whole feeling inside the vehicle to the better. Ever since my first trip to Portland I've tried to greet more and just be a more friendly person when using public transportation.

Here for instance the Austrian Railways has to have campaigns to make people aware that shouting and attacking staff is not acceptable... In [Germany](https://www.derstandard.at/story/3000000307422/nach-gewaltattacke-bei-ticketkontrolle-wie-oebb-und-wiener-linien-ihr-personal-schuetzen) a DB employee has recently been killed!

I'm pretty sure that me greeting public transportation staff won't change the world here, but perhaps it will at least bring a smile to their face and calm someone else down...
