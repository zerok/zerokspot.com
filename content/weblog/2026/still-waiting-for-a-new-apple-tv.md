I've now been "hanging in there" for a new [Apple TV](https://www.apple.com/apple-tv-4k/) for a long time now. Back in 2017, I got the very [first Apple TV 4K](https://everymac.com/systems/apple/apple-tv/specs/apple-tv-4k-5th-generation-2017-specs.html) with the black remote that was and still is really hard to use. Too much touch, too little click 😅 The rumors got really strong throughout 2025 that a new box was imminent, but that never happened. The hold-up seems to be [Siri](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/03/09/apple-tv-4k-new-homepod-launch-timing/). I might be one of the few people out there who really doesn't care about the AI integration there. I use my Apple TV as an Apple Home hub and as the device to watch movies and shows through outside of normal television.

So why am I actually waiting for a new Apple TV box? Well, I could get the version that was released in 2022. It would be completely serviceable for what I'm looking for: a better remote that I can actually also find again when it gets lost on my black sofa and better support for more modern smart-home protocols (i.e. Matter). 4GB of memory instead of 3GB, more disk space, faster CPU; all nice things, but being able to find my remote again and replacing some old smart-home integrations with more modern ones since I use the Apple TV as my Apple Home hub.

But 4-year-old hardware is still 4 years old, and so it makes little sense for me to buy that if a new iteration is around the corner... and has been for a year.
